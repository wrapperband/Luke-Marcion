Marcion, a 2nd-century Christian teacher, produced a text known as the "Gospel of the Lord," which bears significant similarities to the Gospel of Luke but also notable differences. The central question is whether Marcion's Gospel is an abridged version of Luke or if Luke expanded upon Marcion's text.

Marcion's Gospel: A Brief Overview

Marcion's Gospel lacks several elements present in canonical Luke, including:

  • Infancy Narratives: The birth and early life of Jesus are absent.

  • Old Testament References: Passages connecting Jesus to Jewish prophecy and law are omitted.

  • Post-Resurrection Appearances: Certain accounts of Jesus appearing after his resurrection are missing.

These omissions align with Marcion's theological stance, which rejected the Old Testament deity and sought to present Jesus as the messenger of a higher, previously unknown God.

Canonical Luke: Additions and Redactions

If we consider the possibility that the author of Luke expanded upon Marcion's Gospel, the following sections can be seen as additions:

  1. Infancy Narratives (Luke 1–2): The detailed accounts of the births of John the Baptist and Jesus, including genealogies and prophecies.

  2. Integration with Jewish Tradition: Passages that emphasize Jesus' fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies and his adherence to Jewish customs.

  3. Post-Resurrection Narratives (Luke 24): Detailed stories of Jesus' appearances to his disciples after his resurrection, such as the encounter on the road to Emmaus.

Conclusion
